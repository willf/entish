A psalm for Tom Fox

By willwhim

March 11, 2006

The Light is before me, and in me, and surrounds me. The Light soothes my fears and eases my tremblings. I waited before the powerful. I sought to stand in the way of great oppression, Aware that brutality desires hidden corners and not Light.

Lesser oppressors came and surrounded me as well. Surrounded my friends, and me. Yet I held out the Light, even as they hid us, And pursued justice in places of fear and anger. What are the thoughts of my killers as they come to slay me? Do they think their small violence will stop their oppression? Does violence so cloud their hearts that no Light can enter?
